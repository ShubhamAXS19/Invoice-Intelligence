# Common GST Invoice Violations and Validation Rules

## Critical Violations (invoice must be rejected)
1. Missing GSTIN: Every registered supplier must display GSTIN on invoice
2. Invalid GSTIN format: Must be exactly 15 characters matching pattern [0-9]{2}[A-Z]{5}[0-9]{4}[A-Z]{1}[1-9A-Z]{1}Z[0-9A-Z]{1}
3. Missing invoice date: Invoice date is a mandatory field
4. Missing invoice serial number: Must be unique within a financial year
5. Missing taxable value: Base amount before tax must be stated explicitly
6. Incorrect tax calculation: CGST + SGST must equal total GST; each must be exactly half of applicable rate
7. Missing HSN/SAC code: Required based on annual turnover threshold

## Moderate Violations (invoice needs correction)
1. Address incomplete: State name and state code must be present for inter-state supply
2. Missing description of goods/services: Cannot just state "goods" or "services"
3. No mention of reverse charge applicability
4. Place of supply missing for inter-state transactions

## Invoice Total Validation Formula
Expected Total = Taxable Amount + (Taxable Amount × CGST Rate/100) + (Taxable Amount × SGST Rate/100)
Tolerance: ±Re. 1 rounding difference is acceptable

## Date Validation Rules
- Invoice date cannot be a future date
- Invoice date must be within the current financial year for ITC claims
- Indian financial year runs from April 1 to March 31
- Date format on invoice: DD/MM/YYYY or DD-MM-YYYY or DD.MM.YYYY are all acceptable

## B2B vs B2C Invoice Rules
- B2B (Business to Business): Recipient GSTIN mandatory, full tax invoice required
- B2C Large (value > Rs. 2.5 lakh, inter-state): Invoice serial number must be reported in GSTR-1
- B2C Small: Consolidated invoice allowed, recipient details not mandatory
